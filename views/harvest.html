Harvest

Honey harvesting is a crucial part of beekeeping that involves collecting the honey produced by bees after the honeycomb cells are fully capped with beeswax. This process ensures that the honey is ripe and has the correct moisture content, usually around 17-18%, preventing fermentation. The best time to harvest is late summer or early fall, after the honey flow season, and during warm, sunny days when the bees are most active outside the hive. The procedure starts with preparing the necessary tools, such as a bee suit, smoker, and honey extractor, to safely access the hive. After calming the bees with smoke, the frames filled with honey are removed, and the honey is extracted using a spinning tool before being filtered and stored in clean jars.

To have a successful harvest, it’s important to ensure the hive is healthy, with enough honey left behind for the bees’ survival, typically 40-60 pounds. Additionally, minimizing hive disturbance and using clean equipment are key factors in maintaining the hive's health and producing high-quality honey. Understanding the ideal conditions and best practices, such as rotating frames and using a honey extractor, allows for a smooth and productive harvest.
